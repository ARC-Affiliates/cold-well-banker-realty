How Does the Tampa Home Buying Process Work Step by Step?

The Tampa home buying process moves through six standard phases over 30–60 days from accepted offer to closing.

  1. Step 1: Buyer Consultation & Pre-Approval — Meet with a Tampa agent and obtain a mortgage pre-approval letter, typically within 3–7 business days.
  2. Step 2: Home Search & Showings — Tour properties in target neighborhoods; relocation buyers often compress this to one 2–4 day trip.
  3. Step 3: Offer & Contract — Submit a Florida REALTORS®/Florida Bar contract with earnest money, typically 1–3% of purchase price.

What Is the Difference Between a Realtor and a Real Estate Agent in Tampa?

A real estate agent is anyone with an active Florida license to facilitate property transactions; a REALTOR® is an agent who is also a dues-paying member of the National Association of REALTORS®.

All REALTORS® are agents, but not all agents are REALTORS® — the designation requires NAR membership and Code of Ethics compliance.

REALTOR® vs. real estate agent: A REALTOR® is an advantage because the agent agrees to a 17-article Code of Ethics and gains access to NAR resources, MLS systems, and continuing education. A non-member agent is a tradeoff because they may charge lower fees but lack the ethics framework and standardized forms. Jessica McKiverkin Realty operates as a Florida-licensed brokerage whose agents hold REALTOR® designation through the Greater Tampa REALTORS® association. Top realtor Tampa FL professionals often pursue additional designations like CRS (Certified Residential Specialist) or ABR (Accredited Buyer's Representative), which require documented transaction volume and advanced coursework.

Tampa Real Estate Myths vs. Facts

Myth: All real estate commissions are 6% and non-negotiable.

Fact: Following the 2024 NAR settlement, all commissions are individually negotiated and disclosed in writing.

Myth: You don't need an agent when buying new construction from a builder.

Fact: The builder's representative works for the builder; a buyer's agent represents you at no direct cost in most cases.

Myth: Zillow's Zestimate is an accurate Tampa home value.

Fact: Zestimates have a median error of 2–7% on on-market homes and higher off-market — a local CMA is more accurate.

Myth: Tampa flood insurance is only required if you're on the water.

Fact: FEMA flood zones extend inland along the Hillsborough River and into many neighborhoods — check the FEMA Map Service Center.

Myth: Spring is always the best time to buy in Tampa.

Fact: Spring brings more inventory, but late summer often delivers better buyer leverage and lower competition.
